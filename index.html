Improving the Spread of Ideas:

One of the key points we left off at during our last meeting was a request for more freedom to adopt new ideas in the work place. Aaron had mentioned the following technology, innovated elsewhere but taken to its maximum potential in the heart of the Silicon Valley:

What you see in the above image is an example of a place for both creative freedom and modularization. It is this latter point which we wish to now hit home. One such item, a single sticky note, is useful for conveying one idea directly between coworkers. However, after examining the leading forecast of our primary contenders, we have decided to implement a greater strategy, which also involves the following:

From our research team headed by Thwo and Vig, we have reason to believe that though drawing directly on this device can aid complex reasoning, that if we instead draw on these "sticky notes" and then put them on this white board, that productivity increases by nearly two-fold.

What should be noted, as mentioned earlier, is how individual ideas can now be put in a space where all can view them. This creates a potential network through which information can flow and interact, yielding a space that is then in turn democratized by this revolution of discussion and decision-making.

As Phar had brought up last time, by putting all our ideas here, we can then decide on the best paths forward by collectively examining sticky notes posted on this board.

It should be noted, however, that we are still working both the sticky notes and required pens into our budget. 2. The Issues Currently Facing Us:

New Projects Just in:

We have recieved two new projects:

  1. Music and Machine Learning
  2. Garbage Disposal

The first of these has been handed to us by DubSteppers4Lyfe Recording Studios, Ltd. They wish to take hits of previous decades with which to test against incoming songs. The idea is to design a system through which they release the next greatest hits, and maximize their profits. (In case any members of our teams need reminding, this would be a very good thing for us.) They have offered us 3% of the first three hits they make, with further revenue still in negotiation. Considering the situation we find ourselves in, this is quite a good deal.

In particular, they would like us to construct our learning system based on the following classics:
  1. Wake Me Up Before You Go-Go
  2. Never Gonna Give You Up
  3. Working in a Coal Mine
  4. Technologic

The recording studio wishes we priortize our algorithm to similarities with the songs in the above order. Additionally, we are in communication with them about getting more material, since we realize there are too few songs here to reasonably build from. They are in the process of retrieving more, but have voiced their concern over having these emphasized above all else.

The second project could likely be one of our more long-term, fruitful endeavours. We have been reached out to by a utility company for writing an application through which they may optimize their garbage trucks' arrival and delivery times.

The company currently has only five vehicles and three staff members, but should this start-up prospect gain even 1% of the market, we stand to see revenue of the likes we have never seen.
